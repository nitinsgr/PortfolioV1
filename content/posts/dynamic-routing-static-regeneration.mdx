## Introduction
<p className=" font-Fira_Sans text-2xl p-8 text-gray-500">TableTurn aims to tackle the widespread issue of long wait times and limited seating in restaurants, particularly prevalent in high-traffic areas. This project seeks to develop a mobile application that empowers both diners and restaurants to navigate busy environments effectively, leading to a more efficient and enjoyable dining experience for everyone.</p>

## Context

<p class=" text-gray-800 dark:text-gray-200 mt-4">The widespread issue of long wait times and inefficient systems in restaurants is a well-documented problem.  These challenges can create frustration and inconvenience for diners, impacting their overall experience. Recognizing the potential impact on both customers and restaurants.. The scenario leads to framing and thinking problems such as</p>


<ul class="list-disc max-w-3xl ml-4 space-y-2">
    <li class="text-gray-800"><span className=" font-bold">Limited access to real-time information</span>: Users lack transparency about a restaurant's current occupancy, leading to uncertainty and potentially wasted time if they arrive at a full restaurant.</li>
    <li class="text-gray-800">Inefficient waitlist management: Traditional physical waitlists can be cumbersome, lacking real-time updates and user control over their wait time.</li>
    <li class="text-gray-800">Potential for order errors and delays: Traditional ordering methods can be prone to errors and delays, especially during peak hours.</li>
    <li class="text-gray-800">Limited user control over the experience: Users often have limited control over the wait time and efficiency of their dining experience.</li>
</ul>
